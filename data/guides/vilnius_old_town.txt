SITE: Vilnius Old Town
TYPE: Historical district
UNESCO World Heritage Site: Yes, since 1994

OVERVIEW:
Vilnius Old Town is one of the largest and best-preserved medieval old towns in
Northern Europe. It covers 3.6 square kilometres and contains an exceptional
collection of Gothic, Renaissance, Baroque and Classical buildings. The Old Town
has been shaped by many cultures including Lithuanian, Polish, Jewish, Russian
and German influences over centuries.

HISTORICAL BACKGROUND:
Vilnius was founded in the early 14th century. According to legend, Grand Duke
Gediminas dreamed of an iron wolf howling from a hilltop and was told by a
priest that this meant he should build a great city there. Vilnius became the
capital of the Grand Duchy of Lithuania, one of the largest states in medieval
Europe, stretching from the Baltic to the Black Sea.

KEY HISTORICAL PERIODS:
- 1323: First written mention of Vilnius in Gediminas letters
- 14th-16th century: Capital of the Grand Duchy of Lithuania
- 1569: Union of Lublin - Lithuania and Poland form the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth
- 1795: Russian Empire takes control of Vilnius
- 1918: Lithuania declares independence
- 1940-1990: Soviet occupation with brief Nazi German occupation 1941-1944
- 1990: Lithuania declares restoration of independence
- 1991: International recognition of Lithuanian independence

KEY LANDMARKS FOR SCHOOL VISITS:
Gediminas Tower: The remaining tower of the Upper Castle, symbol of Vilnius.
Offers panoramic views of the city. Great for understanding medieval fortifications.

Cathedral Square: The heart of Vilnius. The Cathedral is the main Catholic church
of Lithuania. Look for the miracle tile - a small tile marked "stebuklas" where
the Baltic Way ended in 1989.

Gate of Dawn: The only remaining city gate of Vilnius. Contains a famous chapel
with a miraculous image of the Virgin Mary venerated by both Catholics and
Orthodox Christians.

St. Anne's Church: A stunning example of Gothic architecture. Napoleon reportedly
wanted to carry it back to Paris in the palm of his hand.

Vilnius University: One of the oldest universities in Northern Europe, founded
in 1579. Its courtyards are beautiful and open to visitors.

Užupis: A bohemian self-declared "republic" within Vilnius with its own
constitution. Fun and imaginative for school groups.

EDUCATIONAL THEMES:
- Medieval city planning and architecture
- The Grand Duchy of Lithuania and its multicultural heritage
- Religious tolerance and diversity in historical Lithuania
- Soviet occupation and the road to independence
- The Baltic Way - human chain for freedom in 1989

AGE-APPROPRIATE CONTENT:
Primary (ages 7-10):
- Iron wolf legend of Vilnius founding
- Gediminas Tower and what castles were used for
- Finding the miracle tile in Cathedral Square
- Spotting different architectural styles

Middle school (ages 11-14):
- The Grand Duchy of Lithuania - how big was it?
- Why was Vilnius called the Jerusalem of Lithuania?
- Life under Soviet occupation
- The Baltic Way - why did people hold hands across three countries?

PRACTICAL INFORMATION FOR SCHOOL GROUPS:
Best season: May to September
Recommended duration: Full day (6-7 hours)
Walking distance: Most sites within 2km of each other
Facilities: Toilets available at Cathedral, museums, cafes
Accessibility: Mostly flat, some cobblestones
Nearest train station: Vilnius railway station (15 min walk)
Group discounts: Available at most museums with advance booking

SAFETY AND BEHAVIOUR NOTES:
- Remind pupils to stay together in crowded Cathedral Square
- Quiet and respectful behaviour required in churches
- Cobblestone streets - suitable footwear recommended
- Stay on designated paths near Gediminas Tower hill
