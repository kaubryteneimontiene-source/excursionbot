SITE: Trakai Island Castle
TYPE: Medieval castle and museum
Location: Trakai, 28km west of Vilnius

OVERVIEW:
Trakai Island Castle is one of Lithuania's most iconic landmarks and the only
island castle in Eastern Europe. Built on a small island in Lake Galvė, it
is surrounded by beautiful lake scenery and connected to the shore by wooden
bridges. The castle is a symbol of the medieval power of the Grand Duchy of
Lithuania and today houses a historical museum.

HISTORICAL BACKGROUND:
Trakai was the first capital of Lithuania before Vilnius. The island castle was
built in the late 14th and early 15th centuries by Grand Duke Vytautas the Great,
one of the most powerful rulers in Lithuanian history. After the Battle of
Grunwald in 1410, where the joint Polish-Lithuanian forces defeated the Teutonic
Knights, Trakai became an important political centre.

KEY HISTORICAL PERIODS:
- 13th century: First wooden fortifications on the peninsula
- 1362: Kęstutis builds the first stone castle on the island
- Late 14th - early 15th century: Vytautas the Great expands the castle
- 1410: Battle of Grunwald - Vytautas leads Lithuanian forces to great victory
- 16th-17th century: Castle falls into decline after capital moves to Vilnius
- 1961: Major restoration of the castle completed
- Today: Museum and major tourist attraction

KEY FEATURES FOR SCHOOL VISITS:
The Island Castle: Built on a small island, connected by wooden bridges.
The main tower is 4 floors tall with walls up to 3 metres thick.

Castle Museum: Contains medieval armour, weapons, coins, ceramics and items
showing daily life in medieval Lithuania. Excellent for hands-on history learning.

Lake Galvė: The castle is surrounded by 21 islands. Beautiful scenery that
helps pupils understand why this was a strategic defensive location.

Karaim Settlement: Trakai is home to the Karaim community, brought to Lithuania
by Vytautas from Crimea in the 14th century. They have their own language,
religion and culture. A fascinating example of Lithuanian multicultural history.

Karaim Kenesa: The Karaim prayer house, one of very few in the world.

EDUCATIONAL THEMES:
- Medieval castle architecture and defensive strategies
- Vytautas the Great and the height of Lithuanian power
- The Battle of Grunwald 1410
- The Karaim minority - multiculturalism in medieval Lithuania
- Lake and island geography

AGE-APPROPRIATE CONTENT:
Primary (ages 7-10):
- What was life like in a castle?
- Why did they build the castle on an island?
- Knights and armour in the museum
- The story of Vytautas the Great
- Karaim food (kibinai pastries) - try the local specialty!

Middle school (ages 11-14):
- Castle architecture - how did defensive features work?
- The Battle of Grunwald - why was it important?
- Who were the Teutonic Knights and why did they threaten Lithuania?
- The Karaim people - why did Vytautas bring them to Lithuania?
- Primary sources in the museum - what can objects tell us about history?

PRACTICAL INFORMATION FOR SCHOOL GROUPS:
Distance from Vilnius: 28km, approximately 40 minutes by bus
Best season: April to October (castle exterior beautiful year-round)
Recommended duration: Half day (3-4 hours) or full day with lake activities
Group discounts: Available with advance booking, educational programmes offered
Facilities: Toilets in castle, cafes and restaurants in Trakai town
Nearest transport: Regular buses from Vilnius bus station

SAFETY AND BEHAVIOUR NOTES:
- Wooden bridges - do not run or push on bridges
- Castle towers have steep stairs - supervise younger pupils carefully
- Lake shore - stay away from water edge without supervision
- Museum items - do not touch exhibits unless instructed
