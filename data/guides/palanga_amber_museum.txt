SITE: Palanga Amber Museum
TYPE: Natural history and cultural museum
Location: Palanga, in the Tiškevičiai Palace and botanical garden

OVERVIEW:
The Palanga Amber Museum is one of the finest amber museums in the world,
housed in a beautiful 19th century Neo-Renaissance palace surrounded by a
romantic botanical garden. The museum contains over 28,000 amber specimens
including insects, plants and other organisms preserved in amber millions of
years ago, as well as magnificent amber jewellery and art objects spanning
thousands of years. Amber has been central to Lithuanian and Baltic culture
since prehistoric times.

WHAT IS AMBER:
Amber is fossilised tree resin, mostly from ancient coniferous forests that
grew in the area now covered by the Baltic Sea approximately 44 million years
ago. The Baltic region produces about 80% of the world's amber. Lithuanian
and Baltic amber is known as succinite and has been traded across Europe since
prehistoric times. The ancient trade route along which amber was transported
from the Baltic to the Mediterranean is called the Amber Road.

KEY FEATURES FOR SCHOOL VISITS:
Amber with Inclusions: The most spectacular exhibits are pieces of amber
containing perfectly preserved insects, spiders, plant fragments and even
small lizards that became trapped in the resin millions of years ago.
This is real prehistoric life preserved in perfect detail.
